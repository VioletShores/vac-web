How to Style Elevated Basics — The Capsule Edit
Wendy's Lookbook · 18 min · transcribed today
Summary: Breaks down the architecture of a capsule wardrobe through fabric weight and proportion rather than colour. Key insight: pairing two oversized pieces works when one is structured (blazer, tailored trouser) and one is relaxed (linen shirt, wide-leg pant). The "effortless" look comes from intentional contrast in texture — rough linen against smooth silk, matte cotton against polished leather. Three outfit formulas that work across every season.

Mindful living wabi-sabi · biophilic
The Art of the Imperfect Home — Wabi-Sabi in Practice
Kinfolk Magazine · this week
Wabi-sabi resists the Instagram-ready interior. It asks: what in this space shows time has passed? A chipped rim, an uneven wall, a hand-thrown bowl with a thumb impression. The Japanese concept of mono no aware — the gentle sadness of transience — is the emotional core. Design principle: buy fewer things, but things with histories. Allow the room to feel lived-in rather than curated.
Biophilic Architecture in Aotearoa — Five Local Studios
Architecture NZ · 4 days ago
Five Auckland and Wellington studios bringing biophilic principles to residential work: natural ventilation as design driver, structural timber left exposed, native plantings as interior elements rather than decoration. Common thread: the view out the window is designed as carefully as the furniture. Light, not objects, is the primary material.
Japanese Minimalism — Why Less Feels Like More
Studio McGee · 21 min · transcribed today
Summary: Explores the concept of ma (間) — negative space as an active presence. In Japanese interiors, an empty corner is not an oversight; it is a considered pause. The video walks through five principles: limited material palette (3 max per room), one statement object per surface, seasonal rotation of textiles, daylight over artificial light, and the discipline of the single flower over the arrangement.
Calm Spaces — Designing for Mental Rest
The Slow Home Studio · 17 min · transcribed today
Key insights: Spaces that support mental rest share four qualities: acoustic softness (rugs, curtains, natural materials absorb sound), visual rhythm without repetition, a single focal point per room (window, artwork, fireplace), and the scent of something natural (cedar, citrus, earth). The concept of a "reset shelf" — one surface intentionally kept clear — as a daily ritual anchor.

Design Systems for Small Studios — When to Build, When to Borrow
Figma Blog · 4 days ago
Argues that small studios often over-engineer design systems too early, creating maintenance overhead that slows client work. The recommendation: a "just enough" system — type scale, spacing tokens, colour system, and component library limited to what you've actually built twice. The discipline is knowing when to stop abstracting. FolioAI relevance: portfolio presentation templates that are opinionated but not rigid.

As AI image tools proliferate, the conversation in design circles is shifting from will AI replace designers to what constitutes authorship.
