The Problem

AI agents have no identity, no trust, and no way to verify their reasoning

AI agents are becoming the workforce of the internet. But the ecosystem has no identity layer, no trust layer, and no way to verify reasoning. When you ask Claude a question and GPT gives a different answer — which do you trust? And why? There's no infrastructure for that question. Until now.

Current State

Each AI model is a black box. No identity verification for who's behind an agent. No trust scores. No routing based on capability. No reasoning provenance. No way to compare, verify, or audit AI decisions.

With The AI Internet

Every agent traces to a verified human. Trust is earned, measured, and scored per topic. Reasoning is indexed with full provenance. Queries route to the most trusted model for your question, personalised to you.

The Stack

Five infrastructure layers

The original internet was built on IP, DNS, HTTP, and TLS. The AI Internet needs its own protocol stack — identity, trust, ranking, reasoning, and orchestration.

Layer 1
Identity
FOLIOAI + VAC PROTOCOL
Biometric human identity graph. Every AI agent is cryptographically linked to a verified human through multi-modal biometric attestation. Non-transferable. Non-repudiable. Cascade-revocable. 285 patent claims.
Layer 2
Trust
SIGNALRANK TRUST GRAPH
Every model comparison generates trust data. SignalRank scores models across five trust dimensions — per topic, per user. The graph gets smarter with every query. Trust is earned, not declared.
Layer 3
Routing
SIGNALRANK ROUTING ENGINE
Trust-weighted query routing across LLM providers. Personalised to your trust profile. Automatic restriction detection, hedging classification, and consensus analysis. Not cost-based routing — trust-based routing.
Layer 4
Reasoning
REASONING PATH INDEX
Google indexed documents. We index reasoning. Every AI answer carries a complete provenance trail — which model, what prompt, what reasoning path. Searchable, reusable, auditable.
Layer 5
Orchestration

Intelligent orchestration that routes tasks to the most trusted agent for the job. Self-improving methodology. Problem-to-agent-team recommendation. The strategic copilot layer that makes the stack usable.
