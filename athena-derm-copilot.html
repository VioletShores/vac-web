Research — AI in Dermatology
PathAI's PathAssist Derm tool is showing dermatopathologist-level accuracy in skin cancer diagnosis. The CEO of PathAI believes AI will be incredibly valuable for dermatologists because the specialty relies heavily on evaluating complex visual patterns and tracking how they change over time. The tool is available via the AISight Image Management System. Worth a look — this is the direction your photo triage is heading.
Source: Dermatology Times · Published today
Research — Foundation Models
Mayo Clinic's PanDerm — a multimodal vision foundation model for dermatology developed by Australian and Austrian researchers that integrates whole slide images, clinical images, dermoscopic images, and EHR data. Mayo's DHAI program has built an AI triage system that automatically sorts dermatopathology slides into diagnostic categories, giving pathologists an early preview. This is exactly the kind of tool that could integrate with your practice in the next 2-3 years.
Source: Mayo Clinic Platform
Trend — Teledermatology
2026 is the year digital monitoring platforms go mainstream. Secure image uploads with remote review are now standard across many platforms, and emerging wearable devices may help patients track UV exposure, skin hydration, and environmental triggers with real-time alerts. Your photo triage feature puts you ahead of this curve — most practices haven't started yet.
Source: London Dermatology Centre — 2026 Advances Guide
Watch — Bias in AI Diagnostics
AI skin tools still struggle with darker skin tones. A study found diagnostic accuracy dropped dramatically for Black skin conditions (17%) compared to Caucasian images (70%) when using AI trained predominantly on lighter skin. Important context for any AI diagnostic tool you consider — and an area where your clinical expertise remains irreplaceable.
Source: PMC Systematic Review — 2026
